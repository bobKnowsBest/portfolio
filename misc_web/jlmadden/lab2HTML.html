Education
I hold a Bachelor's of Science Degree in Environmental Science and a Graduate Certificate in GIS. I received my degree and certificate from Iowa State University. I also attended the United States Military Academy at West Point. I have conducted research using LANDSAT imagery that was used to estimate trophic states of various lakes in the state of Iowa. Although my research replicated steps done by other professionals, I was able to produce correlations between actual measurements and estimates of 99%. Traditionally, correlations of 77%-83% were considered acceptable. The higher correlations could be attributed to a number of reasons. First, I used a newer satellite that was in its first year of operation so the satellite might be better suited to the research. The satellite I used was LANDSAT 8. Additionally, the year I estimated values for could have had more homogeneous relationships between the actual and estimated values. One other reason could be that the equation I used was only in its first year of operation. For the earlier satellites a standard equation was used for every year. Perhaps the USGS should produce an equation each year. Nonetheless, my research was notable but further work must be done before any conclusions can be made.

Mobile GIS
Mobile GIS is any spatial data software that can be done from a mobile platform. This can be maps you might be using on your tablet or phone. Additionally, mobile GIS platforms are used by industries across the board. Transportation industries are finding more of a need for mobile capabilities with every day. The military is seeing an increase in their demand for spatial experts as well. Who knows where my research might take me - perhaps with NASA gathering spatial data about our solar system


Automated GIS
Automated GIS is a term floating around more and more offices each day. What is GIS? GIS is any geospatial software. What does automated mean? Automated means making the process of producing something much more simplified and machine run. Automated GIS is helpful when we have to produce repetitive tasks regularly. These repetitive tasks can be weekly maps characterized by the same fields or tables and graphs that get updated regularly. We don't want to automate a tasks that we only do once a year right? In the past I have developed spatial scripts that have reduced the work hours needed to complete a task by 10 hours. Seriously, with the advances we have in computers these days it is possible to condense 10 hour projects into 30 seconds of wonder! It is a skill many disregard but it is very beneficial.

West Point
West Point sits along the bank of the Hudson River. The academy is located at the river's most narrow spot. It is for this reason that what we know as West Point came to be. Back in George Washington's days, when we were fighting the British Empire we found ourselves in need of a base north of NYC. Many of Washington's trusted advisors believed an attack would come from the Hudson so they needed an area to fight along the river. Sadly, Washington's advisors proved to be wrong, wasting tons of colonial dollars for something that provided no benefit. Today the academy is a breeding ground for future Army Officers. The academy boasts one of the worst college football teams ever in the history of sports but it does produce quality americans devoted to serving the nation. I stayed at West Point for almost two years before transferring to Iowa State.
